👑 #1
M

Microsoft SharePoint

Very High Adoption Auth: Moderate

"Index your entire intranet — pages, documents, and permissions-aware content at enterprise scale"

  • Full-text search across SharePoint sites, pages, lists, and document libraries
  • Supports Private, Shared, and Centralized (permission-mirroring) modes
  • Indexes .docx, .xlsx, .pptx, .pdf, and HTML page content
  • Configurable site scope — all sites or specific site collections
  • Daily sync with incremental updates
Why #1

SharePoint is the de facto intranet and document repository for Fortune 500 companies running Microsoft 365. It holds the highest volume of institutional knowledge of any single system. Centralized mode with permission mirroring makes it compliance-safe for regulated industries.

#2
S

Salesforce

Very High Adoption Auth: Moderate

"Surface live CRM data — accounts, contacts, opportunities, and cases — directly in AI answers"

  • Real-time query of Accounts, Contacts, Leads, Opportunities, Cases
  • Supports standard and custom objects via SOQL
  • OAuth 2.0 per-user auth — mirrors Salesforce permissions
  • No data copy — live API calls at query time
  • Works with Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, and custom orgs
Why #2

Salesforce is the CRM of record for the vast majority of large enterprises. Revenue teams spend enormous time hunting for account context and deal history. ROI is immediate and measurable in time-saved per sales interaction.

#3
S

Slack

Very High Adoption Auth: Easy

"Make years of institutional knowledge trapped in Slack channels searchable and AI-retrievable"

  • Indexes public channels, private channels, and DMs (user-scoped)
  • Per-user OAuth mirrors actual channel access
  • Captures message content, thread replies, and shared file snippets
  • Configurable channel inclusion/exclusion
  • Incremental sync — new messages indexed within hours
Why #3

Slack is where real-time decision-making and tribal knowledge live — often the first place institutional memory forms before it reaches a wiki. Easy OAuth and high employee adoption make it one of the fastest connectors to deploy and justify.

#4
G

Google Drive

Very High Adoption Auth: Moderate

"Index Docs, Sheets, Slides, and PDFs across your entire Google Workspace with permission fidelity"

  • Indexes Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, Forms, and uploaded PDFs
  • Supports Private, Shared, and Centralized modes
  • Centralized mode mirrors Google Drive sharing permissions per user
  • Configurable scope: My Drive, Shared Drives, or specific folders
  • Daily sync with delta updates for changed files
Why #4

Google Workspace is the productivity suite for a large share of tech-forward enterprises. Without it, Sana cannot answer questions about any Google-native content. Three integration modes give admins flexibility to match their security posture.

Confluence

Very High Adoption Auth: Easy

#10
M

MCP Connector

Moderate Adoption Auth: Complex

"Connect Sana to virtually any system via the open Model Context Protocol standard"

  • Connects Sana to any MCP-compatible server (thousands of integrations)
  • Admin-configured; Enterprise-only
  • Supports resource exposure and tool calling (live actions)
  • Enables custom integrations without waiting for native connector development
  • Works with MCP servers for ERL, proprietary databases, internal APIs
Why #10

MCP is the escape hatch for every system Sana doesn't have a native connector for. In enterprise environments there are always proprietary or legacy systems that will never get a first-party connector. MCP solves this permanently — it future-proofs any client's Sana investment.
