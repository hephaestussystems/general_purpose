👑 #1
W

Workday

Essential Auth: Managed

"The connector that turns Sana into a true Workday co-pilot — HR, Finance, Payroll, and Performance without leaving chat"

  • Personal information, compensation, and benefits
  • Time off requests, balances, and time entry management
  • Performance reviews, feedback, and goal tracking
  • Org structure, team info, and coworker directory
  • Talent, referrals, and job openings
  • Payroll, direct deposit, and tax forms
Why #1

Workday is why enterprise clients are buying Sana. This connector bridges the conversational AI layer directly into the Workday Self-Service Agent, giving employees natural-language access to HR and finance data they already have permission to see. For a Workday consulting practice, this is always the first connector to demonstrate — it is the proof point that the entire pitch rests on.

#2
S

Sharepoint

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"Search and summarize the enterprise intranet — policies, procedures, and org docs across every site and drive"

  • List and search across SharePoint sites and drives
  • Find files and folders by site, drive, or name
  • Read full file content — docx, xlsx, pptx, pdf, html, and more
  • Sites.ReadWrite.All scope for broad enterprise access
  • Permission-aware access mirrors SharePoint user roles
Why #2

SharePoint is the de facto document and knowledge repository for every Fortune 500 company running Microsoft 365 — the majority. It holds the highest volume of institutional knowledge of any single system: policies, runbooks, org charts, and project docs. Full read-write scope means Sana can do more than just search — it can read and surface documents at query time.

#3
O

Outlook Email

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"Find, draft, send, and manage emails — plus full contact management and attachment reading"

  • Find emails by sender, recipient, subject, date, or folder
  • Draft emails for user review and send on their behalf
  • Read attachments: pdf, docx, images, html, txt
  • List and update labels and top-level folders
  • List, create, and update Outlook contacts
Why #3

Outlook is the email client for the majority of Fortune 500 companies. This connector enables the entire email workflow — not just reading but drafting, sending, and managing contacts. It bridges Workday employee context with the day-to-day communication layer. Missing from the previous top 10; should not have been.

#4
S

Salesforce

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"Full CRUD on Accounts, Leads, Contacts, and Opportunities — live API calls, no data copy"

  • Find, create, and update Accounts
  • Find, create, and update Leads
  • Find, create, and update Contacts
  • Find, create, and update Opportunities
  • Per-user OAuth mirrors Salesforce permissions exactly
  • Works with Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, and custom orgs
Why #4

Salesforce is the CRM of record for the majority of large enterprises. Full read-write access to the entire sales pipeline with no data copy — every query is live. Revenue teams have immediate, measurable ROI: faster deal context, enriched contacts, and pipeline updates without app-switching.

#5
O

Outlook Calendar

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"Find events, check availability, find meeting times, and create or update calendar events"

  • Find events by time, topic, or owner
  • Check availability for yourself or colleagues by email
  • Find mutual meeting times across multiple participants
  • Create new calendar events
  • Update and delete existing calendar events
  • 12 granular Pipedream tools including recurring event support
Why #5

Outlook Calendar is universal in Microsoft shops. Full read-write: the connector not only reads the schedule but creates and updates meetings on the user's behalf. Missing from the previous top 10. Every meeting-heavy enterprise organization has an immediate use case for natural-language calendar management.

#6
G

Google Drive

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"12 capabilities, 34 granular tools — create, copy, update, and manage files, folders, and comments across Google Workspace"

  • Find, read, create, update, and copy files
  • Create and manage shared drives and folders
  • Full comment lifecycle: create, list, update, resolve
  • 34 granular Pipedream tools covering the entire Drive API
  • Centralized mode mirrors Google Drive sharing permissions
  • Supports My Drive, Shared Drives, and specific folder scope
Why #6

The SharePoint equivalent for Google Workspace shops. With 34 granular tools, this is one of the most capable connectors in the library — not just reading but creating, copying, moving, and managing comments. For clients running Google Workspace alongside Workday, this is the document backbone.

#7
M

Microsoft Teams

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"Index Teams messages, channels, and meeting transcripts — the M365 collaboration knowledge layer"

  • Search messages across public and private Teams channels
  • Read channel chats and thread replies
  • Access meeting transcripts from recorded calls
  • Per-user OAuth mirrors Teams membership and channel access
  • Captures shared files referenced in conversations
  • Pairs with SharePoint for complete M365 coverage
Why #7

For Microsoft 365 organizations — the majority of Fortune 500 — Teams is where real-time collaboration and meeting knowledge live. Pairing Teams with SharePoint gives a complete M365 picture: documents plus conversations. Meeting transcripts alone represent a significant untapped knowledge source that most enterprises are not yet surfacing.

#8
S

ServiceNow

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"Full CRUD on any ServiceNow table — incidents, change requests, CMDB, knowledge base, and service catalog"

  • List ServiceNow tables the user has access to
  • Find records in any table using filters
  • Create new records in any ServiceNow table
  • Update existing records
  • Delete records
  • Common tables: incident, change_request, problem, kb_knowledge, cmdb_ci
Why #8

ServiceNow is the ITSM platform for the majority of Fortune 500 IT organizations. Full table-level CRUD means incidents, change requests, CMDB items, and knowledge articles are all accessible and actionable. A natural bridge to Workday: HR creates an employee record in Workday, IT provisions the account through ServiceNow. A high-value Workday consulting pattern.

#9
S

Slack

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"Search conversations, send messages, and manage channels — institutional knowledge for tech-forward enterprises"

  • Search messages across channels with keyword and filter support
  • Send new messages and reply to threads in channels
  • Create new public or private channels
  • Update channel properties and membership
  • Find users by email or list users in channels

Why #9

Slack is the primary collaboration platform for tech-forward Fortune 500 companies — especially those not fully committed to M365. Full read-write messaging means Sana can surface institutional knowledge from channels and also take action: post updates, create channels, send notifications. High employee adoption makes it one of the fastest connectors to deploy and justify.

#10
J

Jira

Very High Adoption Auth: OAuth

"Full CRUD on issues, epics, sprints, and comments — real-time project visibility without a status meeting"

  • Find issues across projects using JQL-powered search
  • Create new issues with full field support
  • Update issues: assignee, status, priority, labels
  • List, create, and update comments on issues
  • List users and spaces (projects)
  • Managed Jira Cloud supported; OAuth mirrors project permissions
Why #10

Jira is the issue tracker for the majority of Fortune 500 engineering and product organizations. Full read-write means Sana can create issues, update status, and add comments — not just read. A natural Workday consulting pattern: Workday manages headcount and workforce planning, Jira manages what that headcount is actively building.
